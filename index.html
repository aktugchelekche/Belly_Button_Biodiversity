Operational Taxonomic Unit (OTU)

Operational taxonomic unit or OTU is considered as the basic unit used in numerical taxonomy. These units may refer to an individual, species, genus, or class. In numerical methods, the taxonomic units used are constantly not comparable to the formal taxonomic units.

1,458 Bacteria Species 'New to Science' Found in Our Belly Buttons

Instead of taking your fingerprint, maybe police should swab our belly buttons with Q-tips. The idea illustrates a point made by a group of North Carolina-based researchers in their new Belly Button Biodiversity (BBB) project. Last month, the group published results of their first of many experiments, in which they swabbed 60 belly buttons and identified a total of 2,368 species of bacteria. People's individual profiles were snowflake-ily, bacterially unique.
